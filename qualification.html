Qualification

Overview

Champions League qualification is a much more complex route than you may imagine. There are 22 teams that get automatically in to the champions league and the 10 more teams that must go through a qualification process. To make things simpler I am going to explain the three ways to qualify to the champions league in three different sections; automatic qualification, the champions route and the league route.

Automatic Qualification

Teams from the countries with the highest coefficient gain automatic qualification to the champions league. The top four leagues get their top four teams entered straight into the group stages of the tournament. Countries with a lower coefficient may only get one, two or sometimes no teams entered straight into the competition but their teams will be entered in qualification routes. For example, France currently has the 5th best coefficient and they get two teams directly entered into the completion and one team entered into the league qualification route. Countries with low coefficients will only get one team entered into the qualification routes.

Champions Route

This route is for the domestic champions from the leagues with lower coefficients, starting with the ten lowest-ranked nations in the first qualifying round. The five victors are joined by the next 29 lowest-ranked champions for the second stage, with the next three sides added for the third qualifying round. The ten winners then face off in five play-off ties. All ties are played over two legs, home and away. This is meant for the smaller countries and gives their top teams a chance to compete in the competition.
